# Contributing

Thanks for contributing to `awesome-french-robotics`.

This list is not meant to be exhaustive. The goal is to keep it useful, credible, and maintainable.

## Selection rules

- The subject must be directly related to robotics, mechatronics, control, robotic perception, or related embedded systems.
- The resource must have a clear connection to France.
- The resource should be genuinely worth recommending, not just present online.
- The resource must be active, maintained, and publicly accessible.
- Official links are preferred.
- Stable, durable URLs are preferred over annual event pages or temporary marketing pages.
- Descriptions should be short, factual, and non-promotional.
- Each entry should follow the format `- [Name](URL) - Description.`.
- Entries must be sorted alphabetically in the relevant section.

## Usually rejected

- Companies focused only on AI or software with no clear robotics activity.
- Archived, abandoned, obsolete projects, or projects with no usable documentation.
- References that are too generic to be genuinely useful.
- Temporary, broken, poorly redirecting, or tracking-heavy links.
- Duplicates.
- Long, vague, or marketing-style descriptions.

## Before opening a PR

- Check that the entry is not already listed.
- Check that the URL works.
- Check that the chosen link is as stable as possible.
- Put the entry in the most relevant section and subsection.
- Keep the scope France-only.
- Be ready to explain in one sentence why the resource belongs in an `awesome` list.

## Pull Requests

- PRs fully generated by AI without human review are not accepted.
- Do not open Draft/WIP PRs.
- A focused PR is better than one mixing many unrelated changes.
- If you add several entries, briefly explain the selection logic and the sources used.
